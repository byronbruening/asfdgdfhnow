Is this the best season in IPL history? Is this the best final in IPL history? If you keep track of the whole tournament and watch last night’s Ruddhaswath final, most of the cricket fans will probably say ‘yes’ to these two questions.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Returning to the home-away format after 4 years after dealing with the shock of Corona, Rinku Singh hitting five sixes in the last five balls to win the team, Virat Kohli regaining himself, Faf du Plessis’ old bone Velki, Yasswi Jaiswal -Akash Madhwal’s rise, Shubman Gill’s incredible form, on the third day Ravindra Jadeja’s steely nerves with 10 runs in the last 2 balls of the made final and Mahendra Singh Dhoni’s surprise to win the title – what was not in this IPL?<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The surprise was also the prize money of the tournament. The Indian Cricket Board (BCCI) has given a record prize money of more than 60 crore 31 lakh rupees in this season, which is more than the last T20 World Cup held in Australia in October-November last year! The ICC gave a total of 60.3 million rupees in financial prizes in the World Cup.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

This year’s champion Dhoni’s Chennai Super Kings got Tk 25 crore 94 lakh, runner-up Gujarat Titans got Tk 16 crore 86 lakh.
